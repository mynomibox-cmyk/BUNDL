🍼 Feeding Your Baby

From first latch to first solids — evidence-based feeding guidance

🤱 Breastfeeding

Breastmilk provides complete nutrition for the first 6 months. Feed on demand (typically every 1.5–3 hours for newborns). Signs of adequate intake: 6+ wet diapers/day and steady weight gain after day 5.

💡 Bundl's sleep data can help you track feeding and sleep patterns together to identify your baby's natural rhythm.

🍶 Formula Feeding

Commercial formula is a safe, nutritionally complete alternative to breastmilk. Newborns typically take 1–3 oz per feed, building to 4–6 oz by 2 months. Never water down formula — it's nutritionally incomplete when diluted.

💡 Track feeding volumes alongside Bundl's SpO₂ data — some changes in oxygen patterns correlate with feeding position.

🥕 Starting Solids

The AAP recommends starting single-ingredient purees around 6 months — when baby can sit with support, shows interest in food, and has good head control. Rice cereal is no longer recommended as a first food.

💡 Introduction of solid foods often coincides with sleep pattern changes. Bundl's trend data can help you distinguish developmental shifts from feeding-related disruptions.

💧 Water & Other Drinks

No water for babies under 6 months — breastmilk and formula provide all the hydration they need. Small amounts of water can be introduced with solid foods at 6 months. No juice under 12 months; limit it significantly until age 3.

💡 Dehydration in infants can affect temperature regulation. If Bundl's temperature readings seem elevated, adequate hydration is worth checking.

🌟 Developmental Milestones

What to expect and when to mention it to your doctor

Remember: developmental ranges are wide. These are general guidelines — every baby develops at their own pace. Always discuss concerns with your pediatrician, not the internet.

1 month

First Responses

Startles to sounds, focuses on faces 8–12 inches away, moves all limbs equally, cries to communicate needs.

Physical Social
2 months

First Smiles

Social smiling begins, begins to follow objects with eyes, makes cooing sounds, holds head up briefly during tummy time.

Social Cognitive
4 months

Active Engagement

Holds head steady, reaches for objects, laughs and squeals, recognizes familiar faces, pushes up on forearms during tummy time.

Physical Cognitive Social
6 months

Sitting & Babbling

Sits with support, transfers objects hand to hand, begins babbling consonant sounds, recognizes own name, starts solid foods.

Physical Language
9 months

Mobility & Object Permanence

Crawls (or finds another way to move), pulls to stand, pincer grasp developing, understands "no", waves bye-bye.

Physical Cognitive
12 months

First Steps & Words

May take first steps, says "mama" and "dada" with meaning, uses cups and spoons, follows simple one-step commands, points at objects of interest.

Physical Language Social
