Why Healthcare Providers Recommend Bundl

Bundl is a wellness monitoring tool — not a diagnostic device. But the longitudinal data it generates has real clinical value when families share it with their care team.

📈

Longitudinal Trend Data

Rather than single-point readings, Bundl provides weeks of continuous heart rate, SpO₂, temperature, and movement data — a picture of how a baby's vitals change over time, not just in-office snapshots.

🛌

Sleep Architecture Insights

Detailed sleep phase data, wake events, and total sleep duration across weeks. Useful for evaluating sleep concerns, colic patterns, and developmental sleep transitions.

😌

Reduced Parental Anxiety

Families with high parental anxiety, NICU graduates, and at-risk infants benefit significantly from continuous monitoring. Reduced anxiety correlates with better follow-through on medical advice.

🤝

Shared Decision-Making

Data-informed conversations lead to better outcomes. When parents come to appointments with 8 weeks of trend data, the quality of clinical discussion improves meaningfully.

👶

NICU Transition Support

For NICU graduates transitioning home, Bundl provides a monitored bridge. Families feel safer, and you receive data that can inform post-discharge follow-up timing and intensity.

📋

Pre-Visit Data Sharing

Families can share a Bundl health summary with your office before the appointment — so you arrive at the visit already familiar with the data and ready to address what matters.

What Data Can Families Share With You?

Bundl's data sharing is parent-controlled and privacy-first. Families choose what to share, when, and with whom — you receive clean, readable reports.

Comprehensive Wellness Reports

The Bundl app generates shareable health summaries that can be exported as PDF or sent directly to a care provider via secure link. Reports include configurable date ranges and all monitored vital categories.

  • Heart rate averages, ranges, and trend lines by week
  • SpO₂ daily averages with below-threshold event log
  • Skin temperature trends with overheating event flags
  • Movement and activity patterns by time of day
  • Sleep architecture breakdown: deep, light, and REM phases
  • Total sleep duration and night waking frequency
  • Alert history with timestamps and parental responses logged
  • Band wear compliance (hours/day monitored)

All data exports include a prominent disclaimer that Bundl is a wellness monitor, not a medical device, and that readings should be interpreted in clinical context.

Clinical Use Cases

How different care teams are incorporating Bundl data into their practice.

NICU Discharge

Post-NICU Home Monitoring

For NICU graduates going home without an apnea monitor, Bundl provides continuous SpO₂ and heart rate awareness during the highest-risk transition period. Families report significantly reduced anxiety, and trend data informs post-discharge follow-up scheduling.

Primary Pediatrics

Enhanced Well-Baby Visits

Pediatricians using Bundl-shared data report that their 2-month, 4-month, and 6-month visits include substantially richer conversations. Sleep data, temperature trends, and movement patterns provide context that single-point office readings cannot.

Sleep Medicine

Infant Sleep Consultations

Sleep consultants use Bundl's sleep phase data to identify wake windows, cycle lengths, and sleep architecture changes over time — giving parents actionable, data-informed guidance rather than generic schedules.

Perinatal Mental Health

Postpartum Anxiety Support

For families experiencing postpartum anxiety (PPA), Bundl monitoring reduces compulsive checking behaviors when parents have access to real data. Perinatal mental health providers can recommend Bundl as a tangible anxiety-reduction tool.
